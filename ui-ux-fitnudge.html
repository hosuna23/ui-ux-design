FitNudge — All-in-One Fitness App UI/UX Case Study

Read more
  • Project: FitNudge — Mobile Fitness Companion App
  • Role: Product Designer · UX Researcher · UI Designer
  • Date: 2025
  • Scope: User Research, Information Architecture, Wireframes, Design System, Figma Prototyping, Accessibility
Objective

Build an all-in-one fitness app that keeps users consistent by unifying movement, nutrition, wellness, progress, and community—reducing app-switching and increasing accountability with personalized nudges.

Problem Statement

People struggle to stay consistent with fitness because tools are fragmented and lack personalization. FitNudge asks: How might we design the ultimate personal fitness companion to support long-term commitment? The solution: a single mobile app combining customized workouts, nutrition, holistic wellness, and community engagement.

User Research Highlights
  • 20 survey participants in 48 hours (IG/Discord/Facebook).
  • 75% female; 50% aged 25–35; 2 with dietary restrictions.
  • 75% train 3–6 days/week; most use a wearable or app.
  • Top goals: build muscle, lose fat, age well.
Frustrations & Pain Points
  • Consistency drops when routines/schedules change.
  • Cost vs. value mismatch; progress not visible.
  • Low customization and app-switching fatigue.
Survey-Driven Insights
  • 35% use fitness apps; 35% use reward systems.
  • 25% have an accountability partner; 15% have a coach.
  • 45% track nutrition; 55% do not.
Personas

Jessica Brown, 29 — Marketing Coordinator (Austin, TX)

  • Motivation: 4–5 workouts/week; mix of lifting & yoga; values journaling.
  • Goals: Build muscle, lose fat, maintain consistency & balance.
  • Pain: Loses motivation without personalization/community; tracking can feel like a chore.
  • Prefs: Journaling, mood tracking, dashboards, reward systems.

Adrian Cruz, 31 — UX Designer (Los Angeles, CA)

  • Motivation: 4–5 lifting sessions/week; loves efficient, clean tools.
  • Goals: Gain muscle, avoid burnout/injuries, stay consistent.
  • Pain: Generic apps; poor wearable sync; over/under-tracking nutrition.
  • Prefs: Clean UI, progress graphs, gym-specific libraries.
MVP — Key Features
  • Onboarding & Personalization: goals, experience, preferences → tailored guidance.
  • Movement: build plans, daily logs, weekly splits, progressive-overload suggestions, wearable sync.
  • Nutrition: macro targets from weight/goals, daily meal logs, weekly check-ins, saved/curated recipes.
  • Wellness: meditations, breathing, journal prompts, sleep tips.
  • Progress: calendar, graphs, trends, algorithm updates, badges & challenges.
  • Community: friends, groups, forums, monthly challenges, playlist sharing.
Naming

After exploring ElevateYou, Self-Evidence, LevelUp, FitPulse, VibeUp, we chose FitNudge—a companion that nudges users with personalized recommendations and accountability.

Information Architecture

Bottom nav: Programs, Progress, Library, Community; top-level Profile. Programs → My Nutrition, My Movement, My Wellbeing. The structure emphasizes personalization, simplicity, and relevance.

View IA in Figma
Process
  • Research → requirements & success metrics (consistency, CTR to logs, challenges joined).
  • IA & user flows → low-fidelity wireframes → high-fidelity prototypes in Figma.
  • Design system foundations: tokens, type scale, spacing, components (variants & Auto Layout).
  • Accessibility: WCAG-aligned contrast, focus states, clear affordances.

Accessibility & Quality
  • WCAG-aligned color contrast, visible focus states, semantic hierarchy.
  • Mobile-first patterns; thumb-friendly tap targets; concise microcopy.
  • Design tokens + components (variants & constraints) for consistency and speed.
Impact

FitNudge consolidates fragmented tools into a personalized, habit-forming experience. The system is ready for usability testing and iteration—built to scale features, reduce app-switching, and improve consistency through nudges, progress feedback, and community accountability.
